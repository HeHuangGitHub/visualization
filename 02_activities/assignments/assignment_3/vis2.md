Visualization 2
He Huang

https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/toronto-island-ferry-ticket-counts/
This dataset provides ferry ticket sales and ticket redemptions.

What software did you use to create your data visualization?
R

Who is your intended audience?
The intended audience is the administration or marketing team of Toronto Island Ferry.

What information or message are you trying to convey with your visualization?
This visualization can show the changes of Redemption and Sales counts of Toronto Island Ferry Ticket among 12 months.

What aspects of design did you consider when making your visualization? How did you apply them? With what elements of your plots?
A pie chart can clearly show the part-to-whole relationships — how each category contributes to a total (100%). I presented the total Redemption and Sales counts of each month in a pie chart to show the proportion.

How did you ensure that your data visualizations are reproducible? If the tool you used to make your data visualization is not reproducible, how will this impact your data visualization?
I used R code to generate a reproducible plot.

How did you ensure that your data visualization is accessible?
The visualization is concisely showing the proportion of the total Redemption and Sales counts of each month in a year with distinguishable colours.

Who are the individuals and communities who might be impacted by your visualization?
The administrators and marketing team of Toronto Island Ferry may see the how the ticket demand change among different months.

How did you choose which features of your chosen dataset to include or exclude from your visualization?
I chose the Redemption and Sales counts. The redemption count shows the real demand of the ferry whereas the sales count is useful for the marketing team.

What ‘underwater labour’ contributed to your final data visualization product?
The 'underwater labour' may include the sale team, the ticket inspectors that recorded the headcount, maybe all the personnel of Toronto Island Ferry as well.
